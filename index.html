National Reference in Computer Networks

The Computer Networks Laboratory is one of the lab of the Institute of Computing at the University of Campinas (UNICAMP), in the State of SÃ£o Paulo, Brazil. Its members conduct research in the different layers of networks: from the MAC layer to the application layer.
The Computer Network Laboratory is co-managed by Prof. Edmundo Madeira, Prof. Nelson Fonseca and other teacher whose hosts graduate and undergraduate students, pos-docs and visitors as well. See more at People to find out more about our team.

Research

The main research interests in the laboratory are listed below.

  • Network management
  • Vehicular Networks
  • Grid computing
  • Optical Networks
  • Network protocols
  • Teletraffic engineering
  • Middleware
  • Sensor networks
  • Wireless mesh networks
  • Wireless sensor networks
  • Software defined networks
  • Intelligence at Networks

People

  • Edmundo Madeira

    He is currently Full Professor at the Institute of Computing (IC) at UNICAMP and a member of the editorial board of Springer's Journal of Network and Systems Management (JNSM).

  • Nelson Fonseca

    Nelson Luis Saldanha da Fonseca received his Electrical Engineer (1984) and MSc in Computer Science (1987) degrees from The Pontificial Catholic University of Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, and the MSc (1993) and Ph.D (1994) degrees in Computer Engineering from The University of Southern California.

  • Luiz Fernando Bittencourt

    He is currently an Associate Professor at the Institute of Computing - University of Campinas. His research interests are on aspects of distributed systems and computer networks, mainly in scheduling and resource allocation in grid/cloud/fog computing systems and their underlying network infrastructure.
